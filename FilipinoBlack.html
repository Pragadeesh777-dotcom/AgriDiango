🌿 Filipino Black Sugarcane Cultivation Guide

1. Land and Soil Preparation

Select deep, fertile, well-drained soils for high productivity.

Plough the land 3–4 times to achieve a fine tilth and remove perennial weeds.

Level the field properly and prepare ridges and furrows at 90–120 cm spacing.

Apply 20–25 tonnes FYM/ha during final ploughing to enrich soil fertility.

2. Soil Type and pH

Best suited soils: Loamy or clay loam soils with good organic matter.

Ideal pH: 6.0 – 7.5.

Avoid waterlogged, saline, or highly alkaline soils.

3. Climate Requirements

Thrives in tropical climates.

Optimum temperature: 22°C – 32°C.

Requires 1000–1200 mm rainfall annually or assured irrigation.

Sensitive to drought → ensure timely irrigation for optimum growth.

4. Seed Variety and Treatment

Variety: Filipino Black Sugarcane (Philippine Black).

Features: Tall (12–20 ft), dark brown–black culms, soft stalks, high juice content, and sweet flavor.

Propagation: By setts (2–3 bud cuttings).

Treatment:

  • Dip setts in 0.25% Carbendazim (Bavistin) or Trichoderma solution for 30 minutes before planting.
  • Alternatively, use hot water treatment at 50°C for 30 minutes to prevent sett rot.

5. Sowing Method and Spacing

Planting season:

  • January–February (Spring planting).
  • July–August (Autumn planting).

Spacing:

  • 90 cm between ridges for sole planting.
  • 120 cm for wide-row planting with intercrops.

Depth: Place setts 10–15 cm deep in moist furrows, cover with soil, and irrigate immediately.

6. Fertilizer Schedule

Basal application: 20–25 tonnes FYM/ha.

Chemical fertilizers (per hectare):

  • N: 200–220 kg
  • P₂O₅: 90–100 kg
  • K₂O: 100–120 kg

Split application:

  • 25% N + 100% P + 50% K at planting.
  • Remaining N and K in 3–4 equal splits at 45, 90, and 120 days.

Apply micronutrients (Zn, Fe, B) in deficient soils for better cane quality and juice sweetness.

7. Irrigation Management

Irrigation interval: 7–10 days in summer, 12–15 days in winter.

Critical stages: Germination, tillering, grand growth, and ripening.

Drip irrigation with fertigation is highly effective for this variety.

Avoid water stagnation to prevent sett rot.

8. Weed and Pest Control

Weed control:

  • Use Atrazine (1.0 kg/ha) or Metribuzin (1.0 kg/ha) as pre-emergence herbicide.
  • Intercultural operations + earthing-up at 90 and 150 days.

Pests: Early shoot borer, top borer, internode borer, white grubs.

Control: Release Trichogramma chilonis, spray neem formulations or recommended insecticides.

Diseases: Sett rot, smut, and wilt (limited reports for this variety, but preventive treatment is advised).

9. Harvesting Time and Yield

Crop duration: 10–12 months.

Harvest when: Canes are hard, mature, and sweet with high juice content.

Average yield: 100–120 tonnes/ha under good management.

Note: Known for multiple ratoon crops → can regrow 2–3 times from the same root system.

10. Post-harvest Handling

Harvest canes at ground level to include the sugar-rich basal portion.

Remove dry leaves and tops; tie into bundles for transport.

Crush canes within 24–36 hours for maximum juice recovery.

Trash and tops can be used for mulching, composting, or cattle feed.
