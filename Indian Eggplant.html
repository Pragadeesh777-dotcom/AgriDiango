🌱 Indian Eggplant (Brinjal) Cultivation Guide

1. Land and Soil Preparation

Initial Preparation: The field should be ploughed 3–4 times to achieve a fine tilth. Remove all weeds and stubbles. Raised beds or ridges and furrows are preferred to improve drainage.

Organic Matter: Apply well-decomposed FYM or compost @ 20–25 t/ha. Incorporating green manure crops before transplanting improves soil fertility.

Bed Preparation: Prepare seedbeds of size 3 m × 1 m × 10–15 cm height. Mulching with organic matter or plastic film helps retain moisture and suppress weeds.

2. Soil Type and pH

Best soils: Sandy loam to clay loam with good drainage.

pH range: 5.5 – 6.5 (slightly acidic to neutral).

Avoid alkaline and saline soils → poor fruit set and quality.

3. Climate Requirements

Temperature: Warm season crop, optimum 25–35°C. Sensitive to frost and extreme heat (>40°C).

Rainfall: Requires 600–700 mm. Heavy rain → waterlogging & flower drop.

Season: Can be grown year-round in India; best in early summer & post-monsoon.

4. Seed Variety and Treatment

Popular Varieties:

  • Pusa Purple Long – long, cylindrical fruits.
  • Pusa Purple Cluster – small, clustered fruits.
  • Arka Nidhi, Arka Shirish, Arka Kusmakar – high yielding & resistant to shoot & fruit borer.
  • Local Desi Varieties – suited to regional tastes.

Seed Rate: 350–500 g/ha.

Seed Treatment: Trichoderma viride (4 g/kg) or Carbendazim (2 g/kg) to prevent damping-off & seed-borne diseases.

5. Sowing Method and Spacing

Seeds raised in nurseries; seedlings transplanted at 30–35 days (4–5 leaf stage).

Spacing:

  • Long-fruited types → 75 × 60 cm
  • Round-fruited types → 60 × 60 cm

Plant population: ~18,000–25,000 plants/ha.

6. Fertilizer Schedule

Basal Application:

  • FYM: 20–25 t/ha
  • NPK: 50:50:30 kg/ha

Top Dressing: Additional 100–120 kg N/ha in 2–3 splits at 30, 60, 90 DAT.

Micronutrients: Foliar spray Boron (0.2%) & Zinc sulphate (0.5%) during flowering → better fruit set.

7. Irrigation Management

Moderately drought-tolerant but requires regular irrigation.

Critical stages: Flowering & fruit development.

Irrigate every 7–10 days (winter) & 3–4 days (summer).

Avoid waterlogging → wilt & root rot risk.

8. Weed and Pest Control

Weed Management: 2–3 weedings essential. Mulching reduces weeds.

Major Pests:

  • Shoot & Fruit Borer – control with pheromone traps, neem oil, insecticides.
  • Jassids, Aphids, Whiteflies – managed with NSKE sprays or systemic insecticides.

Diseases: Bacterial wilt, damping-off, phomopsis blight. Controlled by crop rotation, resistant varieties, fungicides.

9. Harvesting Time and Yield

First harvest: 60–70 days after transplanting.

Fruits harvested tender with soft seeds; delay reduces quality.

Harvest every 7–10 days.

Yield:

  • OP varieties: 25–35 t/ha
  • Hybrids: 50–60 t/ha

10. Post-Harvest Handling

Harvest carefully to avoid bruising.

Sort & grade by size, shape, colour.

Storage: 5–7 days at 10–12°C, 90–95% RH.

Packing: ventilated crates/bamboo baskets.

Value addition: Brinjal pickles, dehydrated, processed foods.

✅ Quick Notes (Revision)

  • Soil: Sandy loam to clay loam, pH 5.5–6.5
  • Climate: 25–35°C, frost & heat sensitive
  • Seed rate: 350–500 g/ha
  • Spacing: 75×60 cm (long), 60×60 cm (round)
  • Fertilizer: 50:50:30 + 100–120 N splits
  • Irrigation: 7–10 days (winter), 3–4 days (summer)
  • Pests: Shoot & fruit borer, jassids, aphids
  • Diseases: Wilt, damping-off, blight
  • Yield: 25–60 t/ha (variety-dependent)
  • Storage: 5–7 days at 10–12°C
