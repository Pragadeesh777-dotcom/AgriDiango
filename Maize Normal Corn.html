1. Land and Soil Preparation

Maize requires a well-prepared seedbed to ensure good germination.

Deep plough the land once with a mouldboard plough followed by 2‚Äì3 harrowings to achieve a fine tilth.

Remove stubbles, weeds, and clods to keep the field clean.

Incorporate 10‚Äì12 tonnes/ha of well-decomposed farmyard manure (FYM) or compost at the final ploughing for soil fertility.

Raised beds or ridges are preferred in heavy rainfall areas to avoid water stagnation.

2. Soil Type and pH

Best soil: fertile, well-drained loamy soils with good organic matter.

Maize is adaptable but performs poorly in saline, waterlogged, or very acidic soils.

Ideal soil pH: 6.0‚Äì7.5.

Soil testing should be done before sowing to adjust fertilizer needs.

Maize is a warm-season C4 crop needing plenty of sunlight.

Optimum temperature: 18‚Äì27¬∞C for germination and growth; up to 32¬∞C during maturity.

4. Seed Variety and Treatment

Popular varieties/hybrids in India: Ganga-5, Deccan-107, HQPM-1, COH(M)6, COH(M)8 (TNAU hybrid).

Seed rate: 18‚Äì20 kg/ha for hybrids, 25 kg/ha for composites.

Seed treatment:

  • Fungicide treatment with Carbendazim/Thiram @ 2‚Äì3 g/kg seed to prevent seed-borne diseases.
  • Biofertilizer inoculation with Azospirillum / Azotobacter for nitrogen fixation.
  • In pest-prone areas, treat seeds with Imidacloprid 5‚Äì10 ml/kg seed to protect against soil insects.

5. Sowing Method and Spacing

Sown directly in the field by dibbling or seed drill.

Spacing depends on variety:

  • Hybrids: 75 √ó 20 cm

Planting depth: 4‚Äì5 cm in light soils, 3‚Äì4 cm in heavy soils.

Ensure uniform plant population of about 60,000‚Äì70,000 plants/ha for good yields.

6. Fertilizer Schedule

Basal dose (before sowing):

  • 10‚Äì12 t/ha FYM
  • NPK (60:60:40 kg/ha)

Top dressing (after sowing): Apply remaining nitrogen in 3 splits:

  • At 30 DAS (knee-high stage)
  • At tasseling stage
  • At grain-filling stage

Total recommended: 150:75:40 NPK kg/ha for hybrids.

Micronutrients like zinc sulphate (25 kg/ha) may be applied in deficient soils.

Foliar sprays of urea + micronutrients improve cob filling.

7. Irrigation Management

Critical irrigation stages:

  • Knee-high stage (20‚Äì25 DAS)
  • Tasseling stage
  • Silking stage
  • Grain filling stage

About 5‚Äì7 irrigations are required in medium soils. Avoid waterlogging, especially during early stages.

Drip irrigation is effective for water-use efficiency and fertigation.

8. Weed and Pest Control

Weeds: Echinochloa, Cyperus, Amaranthus. First intercultivation/hand weeding at 20 DAS, second at 40 DAS. Pre-emergence herbicide: Atrazine 1 kg a.i./ha within 2 days of sowing.

Pests: Stem borer (Chilo partellus), Fall armyworm. Use recommended pesticides and biocontrol.

Diseases: Turcicum Leaf Blight, Downy Mildew, Rust. Grow resistant hybrids + fungicide sprays (Mancozeb 0.2%).

9. Harvesting Time and Yield

Maturity: 90‚Äì110 days (depending on hybrid and season).

Signs of maturity: Husk turns brown and dry; kernels hard and shiny; 20‚Äì25% grain moisture.

Harvest manually or with combine harvesters.

Average yields: Traditional maize: 20‚Äì25 q/ha, Hybrids: 50‚Äì60 q/ha. Fodder yield: 40‚Äì50 t/ha (green fodder).

10. Post-Harvest Handling

Shelling: Done by hand or mechanical shellers.

Drying: Reduce grain moisture to 12‚Äì13% for safe storage.

Storage: Store in clean, airtight bins/silos.

Processing & Uses: Flour, starch, animal feed, ethanol, corn oil, and food products.
