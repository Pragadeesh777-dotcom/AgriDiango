🌿 Sugarcane (CO 18009) Cultivation Guide
🌐

1. Land and Soil Preparation

Select deep, fertile, and well-drained soils for better yield.

Plough the land 3–4 times to achieve fine tilth and remove perennial weeds.

Level the land properly and form ridges and furrows at 90–120 cm spacing.

Apply 25–30 tonnes FYM/ha during final ploughing for improved soil fertility.

2. Soil Type and pH

Best suited soils: Loamy, clay loam, or alluvial soils with high organic matter.

Ideal pH: 6.5 – 7.5.

Avoid highly saline, alkaline, or waterlogged soils.

3. Climate Requirements

Thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Optimum temperature: 20°C – 32°C.

Requires 1000–1500 mm rainfall annually, but performs best with assured irrigation.

Sensitive to prolonged drought → irrigation is essential for better cane yield.

4. Seed Variety and Treatment

Variety: CO 18009 (released by Sugarcane Breeding Institute, Coimbatore).

Features: High sugar recovery, good juice quality, and high cane yield.

Propagation: Setts (cuttings) with 2–3 healthy buds.

Treatment:

  • Soak setts in 0.25% Bavistin solution (Carbendazim) or Trichoderma solution before planting.
  • Alternatively, treat with hot water at 50°C for 30 minutes for disease control.

5. Sowing Method and Spacing

Planting season:

  • January–February (Spring planting)
  • July–August (Autumn planting)

Spacing:

  • Ridges and furrows at 90 cm spacing for normal planting.
  • 120 cm spacing for wide-row planting with intercrops.

Place setts 10–15 cm deep in moist furrows, cover with soil, and irrigate immediately.

6. Fertilizer Schedule

Basal application: 25–30 tonnes FYM/ha before planting.

Chemical fertilizer dose (per hectare): 250 kg N, 100 kg P₂O₅, 120 kg K₂O.

Split application:

  • 25% N + 100% P + 50% K at planting.
  • Remaining N and K in 3–4 equal splits at 45, 90, and 120 days after planting.

Apply micronutrients (Zn, Fe, B) in deficient soils to enhance cane quality.

7. Irrigation Management

Irrigation interval: 7–10 days in summer, 12–15 days in winter.

Critical stages: Germination, tillering, grand growth, and ripening.

Drip irrigation + fertigation is highly recommended for efficient water and fertilizer use.

Avoid water stagnation to prevent root and sett rot.

8. Weed and Pest Control

Weed control:

  • Pre-emergence herbicides like Atrazine (1.0 kg/ha) or Metribuzin (1.0 kg/ha).
  • Intercultural operations (earthing-up at 90 and 150 days).

Pests: Early shoot borer, internode borer, white grubs, and scale insects.

Control: Release Trichogramma chilonis, spray neem oil or recommended insecticides.

Diseases: Red rot, smut, wilt, and sett rot.

Control: Plant disease-free setts, crop rotation, sett treatment, and rogue diseased clumps.

9. Harvesting Time and Yield

Crop duration: 10–12 months after planting.

Harvest when:

  • Canes are hard and mature,
  • Leaves turn yellowish and lower leaves dry.

Average yield: 110–130 tonnes/ha under good management.

Sugar recovery: 11–12% (variety-specific advantage).

10. Post-Harvest Handling

Harvest canes at ground level to include maximum sugar-rich basal portion.

Remove dry leaves and tops, tie canes into bundles for transport.

Crush harvested cane within 24–36 hours for maximum sugar recovery.

Trash and tops can be used for mulching, composting, or cattle feed.
